Spanish

After Portugal's success at traveling across the Sahara desert(see the Portugal slide for more info),
the Spanish thought that they could do the same. This led to them also develop better sailing and
navigating techniques that enabled them to have the ability to compete with the Portuguese at trading.
However, unlike the Portuguese, the Spanish thought that since Earth is round, they could travel
through the Atlantic Ocean to reach Asia. Christopher Columbus, the most famous Spanish explorer,
did exactly that by traveling across the Atlantic Ocean, but he stumbled upon the Americas since
he did not know there's another land in between Europe and Asia. Surprisingly, Columbus found lots
of gold, treasure, and other valuable supplies there, which led to more Spanish expeditions to the
Americas. With the Treaty of Tordesillas(see the Portugal slide for more info), even more Spainish
were driven to migrate to the Americas for permanent settlements. The settlements also allowed
Europeans to have access to more lands, which defined their status, so many Spanish are trying
to get to the Americas as a way to improve their status.

British (Before 13 Colonies)

The British colonies were split into two parts at first when they came to the Americas.
One of them is at the Northeast part of the United States, consisting of modern day Maine,
New Hamphire, Vermont, Massachusetts, Rhode Island, and Connecticut. Another colonization is
at the Southeast part of the Untied States, consisting of modern day Georgia, South Carolina,
North Carolina, Virginia, and Maryland. The British first came to the Americas with the thought
to increase all individual's wealth, power, and status with the gold, silver, and other
treasures that they thought would be free of taking. They also thought that the Native
Americans would see themselves as higher, better beings, which would make them work for the
British willingly. This led to many people, without any agricultural experiences at all, traveling
across the Atlantic Ocean to get to the Americas, where they established the first ever colonization
in Jamestown, Virginia. After their settlement in Jamestown, the British colonists started cultivating
tobacco. The massive tobacco cultivation and its economic success drove even more British to come to
the Americas with the hopes of becoming wealthier.

French

Like all other European countries, France is trying to find a shorter, better route to Asia to access
the Silk Road trades. They traveled across the Americas in hopes of finding the Northwest passage,
a route that directly connects Asia and the Americas through a small path in Northern Canada. As a
result, the French settled in the northern regions of the United States, including the states west
of the Mississippi river and above the Spanish colonization territory, and parts of Canada. Moreover,
the French later found out that the Northern regions of the United States and Canada are inhabited by
a large population of beavers, whose fur are extremely valuable and useful. This led them to establish
trading posts for the animal fur trades, which is a huge economic success for them. Eventually, the
growing fur trade led many more French colonists to come to the Americas. It also made the French
colonies spread across the Americas even more in order to hunt down more beavers(once the hunting region
is low in the number of beavers, the French have to explore more lands in search of more beavers).
However, the fur trade led to a massive reduction in beaver population, impacted the Native Americans
with food depletions, and destroyed various natural environments and habitats.


Dutch

The Dutch came to the Americas mostly to increase the volume of their trades, so they only
colonized New Jersey, Long Island(New York), and Delaware, which were all coastal lands that
are better and more efficient at trading with other countries. Another reason for them to stay
at the coastal regions of the Americas is that in 1492, the Spanish began to determine if people
are Catholic enough or not, which was known as the Spanish Inquisition. If not, then those people
will be forced out of the country. Since most non-Catholics were Jews who were already rich
merchants, they have the wealth to leave and join the Ottoman Empire, who welcomed them
full-heartedly in order to gain access to the Silk Road trades. Since there are already many Jews
in the Dutch colonies, they could just make trades with the Jews in the Ottoman Empire,
eliminating the need to explore the west of the Americas.
Portugal

Due to the fall of Constantinople, the Ottoman Empire now controls most of the trades in the
Silk Road. Since most of the goods with better qualities were traded first in the beginning of
the Silk Road, the European countries at the end of the Silk Road can only receive the ones left
with poorer qualities. This means that they had to pay more money for a lower quality. Ultimately,
this drove Portugal away to find another route to reach Asia to gain more access to the Silk Road.
The lack of trade motivated Portugal to develop sailing techniques that allowed them to sail across
the Sahara desert and straight to Asia, making them the most powerful European country during that
time. However, the Treaty of Tordesillas was established to ensure that both Spain(see Spanish slide
for more info) and Portugal have a monopoly in their trade routes but at the same time reduce some
of the tension and competition between the two strong nations. With the Line of Demarcation established
in Treaty of Tordesillas, the world was split in half with an imaginary line separating the Americas
with Asia, Africa, Europe, and a small portion of Brazil. This allowed the Spanish to gain access to
all the Americas, while the Potuguese can have access to Brazil. This led to Portugal colonizing Brazil
to explore more land and trade. This also made Brazil a Portuguese-speaking country, unlike the other
South American Spanish-speaking countries that it is surrounded by. Moreover, it also started the
Age of Exploration and caused the fall of the Mali Empire.


13 Colonies

The original 13 colonies were the result of the mergence of the two British colonies. After
the British acquired the two lands of the Americas as their colonies, they found out that the
two colonies lacked communication and cooperation. Meanwhile, the other colonies, especially
Spain and France, are growing larger and stronger every day. This urged the British to unify
their two colonies by squeezing out the Dutch colony that's in between their two colonies to
form a better, stronger British colony. As a resullt, the orginal 13 colonies were formed.
The original 13 colonies included Georgia, South Carolina, North Carolina, Virginia, Maryland,
Delaware, Pennsylvania, New York, Connecticut, New Jersey, New Hampshire, and Massachusetts
(which includes Maine back then). All these colonies later became the first few states in the
United States, which is why many Eastern states have British names in them.

Game Description
A game where you can learn the European colonization(Spanish, British, French, and Dutch) of the Americas.

You can also click the checkboxes on the top right corner to show each European colonies.
Click on "M" to access information about the conditions of the Americas before and after European colonization.
